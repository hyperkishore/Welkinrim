Application Note 01 Agriculture Spraying Motors: WR-A2812 · WR-A4010

Motor Selection Criteria

Parameter WR-A2812 WR-A4010
Weight 95g 185g
Max Thrust 1,650g 3,800g
IP Rating IP65 IP65
KV 750 340
Chemical Resistance Yes (epoxy-sealed windings) Yes (epoxy-sealed windings)
Voltage 4S - 6S 6S - 12S

Recommended Configuration

  • Small sprayer (10L tank, <15kg AUW): 6x WR-A2812 on 6S, 13" folding props. Hexacopter for redundancy over crops.
  • Large sprayer (20-30L tank, 25-40kg AUW): 8x WR-A4010 on 12S, 22" props. Octocopter with centrifugal nozzle system.

Performance Stats

At 60% throttle cruise with a 10L payload, the WR-A2812 hex achieves 22 minutes flight time covering 2.5 hectares per sortie. The WR-A4010 octo with 25L payload achieves 18 minutes covering 5 hectares per sortie. Both configurations maintain stable hover in winds up to 28 km/h.

Integration tip: Agriculture motors are exposed to pesticides, fertilizers, and moisture daily. The IP65-rated epoxy-sealed windings on WR-A series motors prevent chemical corrosion of copper. After every 50 hours of spray operations, clean motors with compressed air at 30 PSI max to clear debris from bearing seals.

Integration Tips

  • Mount motors with vibration dampeners to prevent spray nozzle resonance at hover RPM.
  • Route ESC signal wires away from pump power cables to avoid electromagnetic interference.
  • Use conformal-coated ESCs rated for the same IP level as the motors.
  • Schedule motor bearing replacement every 500 flight hours for sustained field operations.

Motor Selection Criteria

IP Rating IP68 IP68
KV 480 220
MIL-STD-810H Compliant Compliant
Operating Temp -40C to +80C -40C to +80C
Voltage 6S - 8S 8S - 12S

Recommended Configuration

  • Man-portable ISR (5-8kg AUW): 4x WR-D3510 on 8S, 15" low-noise props. Quadcopter with folding arms for backpack transport. EO/IR gimbal payload.
  • Extended-range ISR (15-25kg AUW): 8x WR-D5015 on 12S, 22" props. Octocopter or VTOL fixed-wing hybrid for 60+ minute endurance with SIGINT/COMINT payloads.

Performance Stats

The WR-D3510 quad achieves 42 minutes loiter time at 300m AGL with a 1.2kg EO/IR payload on 8S 12,000mAh packs. Acoustic signature at 100m altitude measures below 55 dBA with low-RPM carbon fiber props. The WR-D5015 VTOL platform sustains 90+ minutes total mission time with 45 minutes on-station loiter carrying a 3.5kg multi-sensor payload.

Integration tip: Defence ISR platforms demand silent operation. Use the lowest KV variant available and the largest propeller that fits your airframe. Every 10% reduction in RPM yields approximately 4 dBA noise reduction. The WR-D series N52SH magnets maintain full field strength at 80C continuous, critical for desert deployments.

Integration Tips

  • Use MIL-spec connectors (Amphenol or equivalent) for motor-to-ESC wiring. Avoid solder joints that may crack under vibration.
  • Torque motor mounting bolts to 1.8 Nm with medium-strength threadlocker (Loctite 243).
  • Perform vibration analysis after assembly. Motor imbalance should be below 0.5 mm/s RMS at hover RPM.
  • For salt-fog environments, apply corrosion-inhibiting compound to all exposed aluminum surfaces every 100 hours.
  • EMC shielding: Route encoder/telemetry cables at least 50mm from motor phase wires.
Application Note 03 Commercial Delivery Motors: WR2815 · WR3508

Motor Selection Criteria

Parameter WR2815 WR3508
Weight 82g 125g
Max Thrust 1,420g 2,150g
IP Rating IP65 IP67
KV 900 680
Efficiency 88% 90%
Voltage 4S - 6S 6S - 8S

Recommended Configuration

  • Last-mile light delivery (1-2kg payload, 5kg AUW): 4x WR2815 on 6S, 12" props. Quadcopter optimized for 15-minute round trips within 5km radius.
  • Medium delivery (3-5kg payload, 10-12kg AUW): 6x WR3508 on 8S, 15" props. Hexacopter for motor-out redundancy with payload winch system.

Performance Stats

The WR2815 quad completes a 5km round trip in 12 minutes carrying a 1.5kg package, consuming 38% of a 6S 8,000mAh battery. This allows 2 full deliveries per charge with reserve. The WR3508 hex achieves 25 minutes flight time with a 4kg payload, covering a 7km delivery radius with 20% battery reserve for safe return.

Integration tip: Delivery drones fly hundreds of cycles per week. Motor bearing life is the primary maintenance driver. WelkinRim's ceramic hybrid bearings (Si3N4 balls, steel races) in the WR2815 and WR3508 achieve 2,000+ hours MTBF at delivery-cycle loads, versus 800 hours for standard steel bearings. This reduces per-delivery maintenance cost by approximately 60%.

Integration Tips

  • Size propellers for maximum hover efficiency at 55-65% throttle with full payload. This extends motor and ESC life.
  • Use quick-disconnect motor mounts for field-swappable motor replacement in under 3 minutes.
  • Implement motor temperature monitoring via ESC telemetry. Set a 120C winding temp alarm threshold.
  • For rain delivery operations, ensure ESC compartment matches the motor IP rating. A single unsealed ESC defeats the purpose of IP67 motors.
Application Note 04 Industrial Inspection Motors: WR2212 · WR2815

Motor Selection Criteria

Parameter WR2212 WR2815
Weight 56g 82g
Max Thrust 850g 1,420g
IP Rating IP54 IP65
KV 1000 900
Efficiency 85% 88%
Voltage 3S - 4S 4S - 6S

Recommended Configuration

  • Indoor / confined space inspection (1-2kg AUW): 4x WR2212 on 4S, 9" props with prop guards. Lightweight quadcopter for pipeline interiors, tank inspections, and indoor warehouses.
  • Outdoor infrastructure (2-4kg AUW): 4x WR2815 on 6S, 12" props. Quadcopter with LiDAR, thermal camera, or high-res zoom payload for bridge, tower, and building facade inspection.

Performance Stats

The WR2212 inspection quad achieves position hold accuracy of plus or minus 5cm using GPS-RTK, with negligible motor-induced vibration (below 0.3g at flight controller mount). Hover duration reaches 28 minutes on 4S 5,000mAh. The WR2815 outdoor platform maintains stable hover in 35 km/h gusts while capturing 50MP stills at 30m from target structures, with 25 minutes endurance on 6S 10,000mAh.

Integration tip: Precision hover is non-negotiable for inspection. Motor vibration directly degrades camera clarity and LiDAR point cloud accuracy. WelkinRim motors are dynamically balanced to ISO G1.0 at the factory. After prop installation, perform a hover vibration test and reject any motor-prop combination exceeding 0.5g peak at the FC mount.

Integration Tips

  • Use carbon fiber propellers for inspection work. They are stiffer than nylon, reducing flex-induced vibration at steady hover.
  • Mount thermal cameras at least 100mm from motors to avoid heat interference in close-range inspections.
  • For GPS-denied indoor environments, use optical flow sensors and ensure motor RPM telemetry feeds into the flight controller for altitude estimation.
  • Implement motor current logging to detect early bearing degradation before it causes position hold drift.
Application Note 05 Racing / FPV Motors: WR2212-1400KV

Motor Selection Criteria

Parameter WR2212-1400KV
Weight 52g
Max Thrust 1,100g
IP Rating IP44
KV 1400
Response Time <15ms (0-80% throttle)
Voltage 4S - 6S
Max Current 35A burst

Recommended Configuration

  • 5-inch freestyle / race quad (600-800g AUW): 4x WR2212-1400KV on 6S, 5.1" triblade props. True-X frame with 30x30 stack. Target thrust-to-weight ratio of 8:1 or higher for competitive racing.
  • 7-inch long-range FPV (900-1200g AUW): 4x WR2212-1400KV on 6S, 7" biblade props. Stretched-X frame optimized for forward flight efficiency. 30+ minute cruise range.

Performance Stats

On 6S with 5.1" triblade props, the WR2212-1400KV produces 1,100g thrust per motor, achieving a 5.5:1 thrust-to-weight ratio on a 800g build. Throttle response from idle to 80% completes in under 15 milliseconds. Top speed on a race course exceeds 160 km/h. With 7" biblades on the long-range build, cruise efficiency reaches 8.2 g/W at 60 km/h forward flight, enabling 35 km range on a 1,300mAh 6S pack.

Integration tip: For racing, motor response time matters more than peak thrust. The WR2212-1400KV uses a 14-pole arc magnet design with low-inductance windings, enabling the fastest throttle transients in its class. Pair with 48kHz capable ESCs running BLHeli_32 for the smoothest stick feel.

Integration Tips

  • Use soft-mount motor screws (rubber O-rings under screw heads) to reduce high-frequency vibration transmitted to the gyro.
  • Solder motor wires directly to ESC pads for the lowest resistance connection. Avoid bullet connectors on race builds.
  • Set motor timing advance to 22.5 degrees for optimal efficiency and response on 6S.
  • After crashes, spin each motor by hand and listen for bearing roughness. Replace any motor with detectable bearing play immediately to prevent mid-flight desync.
Application Note 06 Custom Integration Custom Windings · Bespoke Configurations

When You Need Custom

Standard motors cover 80% of applications. For the remaining 20% with unique operating envelopes, payload profiles, or environmental conditions, WelkinRim offers custom motor winding and configuration services. Minimum order quantity for custom builds is 50 units.

Custom Winding Guide

Winding Change Effect on KV Effect on Torque Use Case
More turns, thinner wire Lower KV Higher torque per amp Heavy-lift, large props, efficiency
Fewer turns, thicker wire Higher KV Lower torque, higher RPM Racing, small props, agility
Delta winding (vs. Y) 1.73x higher KV Lower torque per amp Higher RPM on lower voltage
Y winding (vs. Delta) Standard KV Higher torque per amp Standard efficiency operation

How to Spec a Custom Motor

Provide the following information to our engineering team for a custom motor quote. The more detail you share, the faster we can iterate on a design.

  • Target thrust per motor at your operating voltage and desired propeller size.
  • Operating voltage range (minimum and maximum cell count).
  • Propeller diameter and pitch you plan to use, or let us recommend one.
  • Duty cycle: continuous hover percentage, burst duration, cooldown periods.
  • Environmental conditions: temperature range, humidity, altitude, exposure to chemicals or salt spray.
  • IP rating requirement and any military or aviation standards (MIL-STD, DO-160, CE, FCC).
  • Mechanical constraints: maximum motor diameter, height, weight, mounting pattern.
  • Volume: expected annual quantity for production pricing.

Performance Validation

Every custom motor order includes a dynamometer test report showing thrust curves, efficiency maps, and thermal profiles across the full operating range. Prototype samples ship within 4 weeks. Production lead time is 6-8 weeks after design sign-off.

Integration tip: Before committing to a custom winding, test your airframe with the closest standard motor. Measure actual hover current, peak current during maneuvers, and motor surface temperature after 10 minutes of flight. This data tells our engineers exactly where to optimize the winding for your specific operating point.
